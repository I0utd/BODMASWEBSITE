Introduction to BODMAS Rule

The BODMAS rule is a convention used to clarify the order of operations when evaluating arithmetic expressions. BODMAS stands for:

  • B - Brackets
  • O - Orders (Powers)
  • D - Division
  • M - Multiplication
  • A - Addition
  • S - Subtraction

Following the BODMAS rule ensures that mathematical expressions are evaluated correctly, as different operations have different priorities.

Brackets

Brackets are evaluated first in the BODMAS rule. They can be used to group expressions and alter the order of operations.

For example, in the expression 2 * (3 + 4), the addition inside the brackets is performed first, resulting in 2 * 7.

Orders (Powers)

Orders, or powers, are evaluated next. They involve raising a number to a certain power.

For example, in the expression 2^3, 2 is raised to the power of 3, resulting in 8.

Division

Division is the next operation to be evaluated. It involves dividing one number by another.

Multiplication

Multiplication is evaluated after division. It involves multiplying two numbers together.

For example, in the expression 3 * 4, 3 is multiplied by 4, resulting in 12.

Addition

Addition is evaluated after multiplication. It involves adding two numbers together.

For example, in the expression 5 + 2, 5 is added to 2, resulting in 7.

Subtraction

Subtraction is the last operation to be evaluated. It involves subtracting one number from another.

For example, in the expression 8 - 3, 3 is subtracted from 8, resulting in 5.
